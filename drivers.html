Become a HERWAY Driver

Join our women-led UK taxi network focused on safe, professional journeys

Driver Requirements

We're a women-by-women service with a female-first model, and we also assess vetted male drivers where operationally required.

📋

Valid UK License

Full UK driving license with no serious driving violations in the last 3 years.

🚗

Vehicle Standards

Vehicle must be registered and roadworthy. Modern vehicles are preferred (usually 2015 or newer).

🪪

PVH License

Private Hire Vehicle (PVH) license or assistance obtaining one. We can help guide you through the process.

🔐

DBS Check

Enhanced DBS (Disclosure and Barring Service) clearance required. We can advise on the application process.

📄

Insurance

Valid Private Hire Insurance and MOT certificate for your vehicle.

👩

Women-First Driver Policy

Female drivers are prioritized. Vetted male drivers may be approved under the same licensing, DBS, and safeguarding standards.

UK Vehicle Requirements

Our platform vehicle standards are similar to leading ride-hailing operators in the UK and are strictly enforced.

✅

Minimum Vehicle Size

Only 4-door vehicles, minivans/MPVs, or larger vehicles are accepted. This is mandatory for all HERWAY driver partners.

🚫

Vehicles Not Accepted

2-door cars, coupes, convertibles, and small commercial vans are not eligible for HERWAY bookings.

🛡️

Road & Safety Compliance

Vehicle must be roadworthy with valid MOT, correct PHV licensing (where required), and active private hire insurance.

🧼

Condition & Cleanliness

Vehicles must be clean, smoke-free, and maintained to professional passenger standards inside and out.

👶

Family Travel Ready

Vehicle must have sufficient space for child seats and safe passenger boarding when child travel is disclosed.

📅

Vehicle Age Guidance

Modern vehicles are preferred (typically 2015 or newer), subject to local authority private hire standards.

Important: If your vehicle is not a 4-door, minivan/MPV, or larger vehicle, your application cannot be approved.

Application & Verification Process

Follow these steps to become a HERWAY driver:

1

Initial Application

Complete our online application form with your basic information and driving experience.

2

Document Submission

Upload copies of:

  • Valid UK Driving License (front & back)
  • Passport or National ID Card
  • Vehicle Registration Certificate (V5C)
  • Proof of Address (utility bill or bank statement)
  • Insurance Certificate
3

DBS & Background Check

We'll guide you through obtaining an Enhanced DBS check. You may apply yourself or we can assist you.

4

PVH License Requirement

Ensure you have a PVH (Private Hire Vehicle) license. Contact your local council for details. We can provide guidance on the requirements specific to your area.

5

Online Interview

Complete a video interview to discuss your experience, vehicle, and understanding of our safety policies.

6

Document Verification

We'll verify all your documents. This includes checking your DBS clearance, insurance, and vehicle roadworthiness.

7

Safety Training

Complete our online safety training module covering customer service, safety protocols, and our policies.

8

Approval & Onboarding

Once approved, you'll be onboarded to our system and can start accepting bookings!

Our Driver Safety & Conduct Policies

All HERWAY drivers must adhere to strict safety and conduct standards. Drivers have the right to cancel or refuse any trip if:

❌ Undisclosed Male Passengers

A male passenger boards who was not mentioned during booking. Drivers can cancel to maintain our women-focused service commitment.

❌ Passenger Intoxication

Passenger appears intoxicated, aggressive, or unable to communicate clearly.

❌ Inappropriate Behavior

Any harassment, disrespectful behavior, or discomfort for the driver or other passengers.

❌ Route Safety Concerns

The route or destination raises legitimate safety concerns for the driver.

❌ Undisclosed Passengers

More passengers or children than declared at booking, especially without car seats for children.

❌ Vehicle Damage Risk

Passenger appears to pose a risk of damage to the vehicle.

Important: Drivers are never obligated to complete a journey if they have safety concerns. Passenger safety is important, but driver safety is paramount and non-negotiable.
