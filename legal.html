Operating Standards & Regulatory Compliance

HERWAY operates in full compliance with all relevant UK legislation and regulations governing taxi and private hire vehicle services. Below is our comprehensive compliance framework, including all requirements we have verified and implemented.

Compliance Checklist

All items below have been checked and implemented by HERWAY:

🚕 Taxi Licensing & Operations

✓

Private Hire Vehicle (PHV) License

All HERWAY vehicles are licensed as Private Hire Vehicles with the appropriate local authority. Drivers must hold valid PHV licenses.

✓

PVH Driver License

All drivers must possess a valid Private Hire Driver (PVH) license issued by their local council, obtained after passing background checks and knowledge tests.

✓

Operator License

HERWAY holds the necessary Operator License(s) as required by local authorities for operating a taxi/private hire service.

✓

Vehicle Compliance

All vehicles meet strict safety and suitability standards set by local authorities, including accessibility requirements.

✓

MOT & Roadworthiness

All vehicles maintain current MOT certificates with no serious defects. Regular safety inspections are conducted.

✓

Meters & Pricing

We maintain transparent pricing structures and provide accurate quotes to customers before services are rendered.

🛡️ Insurance & Financial Security

✓

Private Hire Vehicle Insurance

All vehicles are insured with comprehensive Private Hire Vehicle insurance policies that cover passengers and third parties.

✓

Passenger Liability Coverage

Full passenger liability insurance is maintained on all vehicles, protecting customers in case of injury or damage.

✓

Third-Party Liability

Comprehensive third-party liability coverage is in place for damage to other vehicles or property.

✓

Driver Insurance Verification

All drivers' insurance is verified regularly and maintained throughout their employment with HERWAY.

✓

Fare Payment Security

Payment processing complies with PCI-DSS standards for customer financial data security.

👮 Background Checks & Safeguarding

✓

Enhanced DBS Checks

All drivers undergo Enhanced DBS (Disclosure and Barring Service) checks required for roles involving frequent contact with the public.

✓

Barred Lists Check

Verification that drivers are not listed on the DBS children's or adults' barred lists.

✓

Driving License Verification

DVLA check on all drivers to verify valid UK driving licenses with no serious violations within the past 3 years.

✓

Reference Checks

Previous employer references are obtained and verified for all driver applicants.

✓

Identity Verification

Proof of identity is verified using government-issued documents (passport/national ID).

✓

Safeguarding Policy

Comprehensive safeguarding policy in place to protect vulnerable passengers, particularly children.

✓

Lone Working Policy

Enhanced safety protocols for drivers, particularly drivers working alone on night shifts.

Regulatory Bodies & Oversight

HERWAY operates under the supervision and oversight of the following regulatory organizations:

Local Authority Licensing

Taxi and Private Hire licensing managed by local council/licensing authority.

Contact your local licensing authority for license verification.

ICO (Information Commissioner's Office)

UK data protection regulator overseeing GDPR and Data Protection Act 2018 compliance.

Website: ico.org.uk

DBS (Disclosure and Barring Service)

Provides enhanced background checks for roles involving public contact.

Website: disclosurebarring.co.uk

Trading Standards

Oversees consumer protection and unfair trading practices.

Website: citizensadvice.org.uk/about-us/our-work/policy/policy-topics/consumer-policy-topics-and-consultation-responses/consumer-policy-topics/trading-standards/

HSE (Health & Safety Executive)

Enforces health and safety legislation in the workplace.

Website: hse.gov.uk

HMRC (HM Revenue & Customs)

Manages tax compliance and employment law enforcement.

Website: hmrc.gov.uk

DVLA (Driver & Vehicle Licensing Agency)

Issues and manages driving licenses and vehicle registrations.

Website: dvla.gov.uk

ACAS (Advisory, Conciliation and Arbitration Service)

Provides employment rights and dispute resolution support.

Website: acas.org.uk
